Abstract

In this project we explored stitching images together to create mosaics. Additionally, we are able to any image and project it onto another plane.

Part 1: Shooting the Pictures

I shot a few snapshots of my life during these past couple of weeks. In order to fix the center of projection, I utilized a water bottle to stabilize my phone while I rotate the camera.

Part 2: Recovering Homographies

Using the student made tool, I selected correspondence points between the images and exported it as a json. In order to warp from one image to another, we need a transformation matrix, in this case, a 3x3 homography matrix to warp points on img1 to img2 .

To find the matrix, I setup a linear system of equations and used np.lingalg.lstsquares to approximate a solution.

Part 3: Warp the Images

To warp my images, I determined the bounding box by applying the homography to the corners of img1. Taking these corner coordinates and feeding into ski.draw.polygon gave me the coordinates inside the bounding box, which I used to inverse warp with the inverse of h to get the warped source coordinates.
